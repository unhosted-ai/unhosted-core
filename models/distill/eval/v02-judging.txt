### 14 [consequence_prediction] (153 tok)
PROMPT: What second-order effects follow from making the on-call rotation opt-in?
OUTPUT: The on-call rotation is no longer a default path to the team; it's a choice. Second-order effects: (1) the opt-out becomes a real decision, not a formality, and people who opt out genuinely disengage from incident response. (2) The on-call team shrinks to those who actually want it, so incident response capacity is now tied to who signed up, not who's available. ### 15 [consequence_prediction] (108 tok)

OUTPUT: The approval step becomes the liability.
