### 3 [logic_math] (205 tok)
PROMPT: A queue processes 240 jobs per hour. Jobs arrive at 300 per hour for 2 hours, then drop to
OUTPUT: The queue's capacity is 240 jobs/hour, and the surge is 300 jobs/hour for 2 hours, then 180 jobs/hour. The surge is 2 hours, so the backlog is 300 - 240 = 60 jobs/hour of surplus, and it builds 2 hours: 60 x 2 = 120 jobs. After the surge, the arrival drops to 180, which is 30 jobs above capacity, so it grows another 30 jobs/hour of backlog. The queue must absorb the 120 jobs of surplus, and the 30-job gap grows continuously. So the time to clear the backlog is the 2 hours of surplus plus the time to absorb the 30-job gap: 2 + (120 / 30) = 2 + 4 = 6 hours. So the answer is: 6 hours.
