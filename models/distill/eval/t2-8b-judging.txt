### 14 [consequence_prediction] (188 tok)
PROMPT: What second-order effects follow from making the on-call rotation opt-in?
OUTPUT: Opt-in on-call rotation means people can choose to sign up, not that it's mandatory. The second-order effects include:

1. Lower coverage: Fewer people sign up, so the team is less available during incidents.
2. Uneven load: Those who sign up bear more than their share, creating burnout.
3. Worse incident response: Fewer people on call means slower detection and resolution.
4. Lower morale: People who don't sign up may feel the team is less reliable, and those who do may feel overburdened.
5. Less knowledge sharing: Fewer people on call means less cross-training and handover.
6. Higher incident costs: Slower detection and resolution mean more damage and longer resolution times.
7. Lower team trust: If some people don't sign up, others may lose trust in the team's reliability.

The tradeoff is between fairness and coverage.
